The Aruna Initiatives

A Bankable Framework for Community-Based Electrification and Indonesia's Blue Economy

Team: Albert Rafael & Moch. Arizal, Universitas Airlangga

WHY: The Challenge & Opportunity

Indonesia's small islands are trapped in a cycle of expensive, polluting diesel power, costing up to 30 times the national average and hindering development. Gili Ketapang, a densely populated island, is a prime example of this challenge, facing both energy and water insecurity.

The government's "dedieselisasi" program and the island's high solar potential create a unique opportunity to build a replicable model for a sustainable transition.

HOW: A Unique Dual-Entity Framework

We de-risk investment and maximize value through two synergistic companies:

1. Renewable & Hydrogen Co. (RHC): An IPP that develops Floating PV and Hydrogen infrastructure, selling power to the state utility (PLN) under long-term, bankable contracts (PPA & IaaS).

2. Flexible Water Co. (FWC): Develops modular desalination plants powered by renewables, selling water to the regional utility (PDAM) through a Build-Lease-Transfer (BLT) model.

WHAT: The Integrated Solution

A resilient, island-wide system combining a 2 MWp Floating PV plant with a 900 kW electrolyzer to produce green hydrogen. This hydrogen supplies a 176 kW Fuel-Cell Generator for reliable nighttime power, all managed through performance-based contracts.

The Aruna Initiatives

A Bankable Framework for Community-Based Electrification and Accelerating Indonesia's Blue Economy

Presenters: Albert Rafael & Moch. Arizal, Universitas Airlangga

The Challenge is Immense. The Opportunity is Greater.

The Diesel Dependency Crisis

Over 2,100 sites in Indonesia rely on diesel generators, creating a massive financial and environmental burden.

  • High Costs: Electricity costs soar to IDR 3,000-50,000/kWh, up to 30x the national average.
  • Carbon Intensive: Emits roughly 3 MtCO₂ annually, undermining climate goals.
  • Vulnerable Supply Chains: Remote islands like Gili Ketapang face constant disruptions.

Gili Ketapang: A Microcosm of Opportunity

This densely populated island is a prime candidate for a renewable transformation, supported by government initiatives.

  • High Solar Irradiation: >4.9 kWh/m²/day, perfect for PV systems.
  • Govt. Support: Aligns with the "dedieselisasi" program and PLN's green roadmap.
  • Urgent Need: Unreliable power and water threaten livelihoods in fishing and tourism.

Our Solution: A Dual-Entity IPP Framework

We designed a bankable, socio-commercial architecture that de-risks investment, aligns stakeholders, and empowers the local community through two specialized entities.

1. Renewable & Hydrogen Co. (RHC)

An Independent Power Producer (IPP) focused on clean energy generation and storage infrastructure.

  • Model: Sells electricity to PLN via a long-term Power Purchase Agreement (PPA).
  • Technology: Floating PV for power, with Green Hydrogen infrastructure leased via an Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS) contract.

2. Flexible Water Co. (FWC)

A specialized utility to provide clean, affordable water for industrial and community needs.

  • Model: Develops modular desalination plants and sells water to the local utility (PDAM) under a Build-Lease-Transfer (BLT) agreement.
  • Innovation: Integrates Waste Heat Recovery (WHR) from the hydrogen process to boost efficiency.

The Integrated Technical Architecture

System Components

  • Floating Solar (FPV): A 2,000 kWp system generating over 3.7 GWh annually.
  • Electrolyzer: A 900 kW unit to produce ~45,000 kg of Green Hydrogen per year.
  • H₂ Fuel Cell (HFCG): 176 kW capacity to cover over 58% of nighttime electricity demand.
  • Desalination (SWRO): A modular 800 m³/day plant, enhanced by waste heat recovery.

Comprehensive Risk Mitigation Strategy

📄
Contractual & Financial

Long-term PPA/BLT contracts with state-owned utilities (PLN, PDAM) secure revenue. A blended finance model minimizes initial CAPEX risk.

🛠️
Technical & Operational

Proven, modular technologies (FPV, SWRO) are used. Performance-based O&M contracts with technology suppliers guarantee uptime and efficiency.

🌍
Social & Environmental

Community ownership models, including women-led cooperatives, ensure local buy-in. The project aligns with national decarbonization goals.

Empowering Communities, Ensuring Sustainability

A Catalyst for Local Prosperity

This is more than an infrastructure project; it's a commitment to community resilience and inclusive growth.

  • Local Ownership: Women-led cooperatives are empowered to manage water distribution and participate in O&M, creating local jobs and value.
  • Improved Health & Livelihoods: Reliable access to clean water and affordable electricity improves health outcomes, supports local businesses, and enables new economic activities.
  • Gender Equity: Prioritizing women’s groups strengthens governance and ensures equitable benefit sharing.

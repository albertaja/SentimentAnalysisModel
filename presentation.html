The Diesel Dependency Crisis

Indonesia operates 5,200 diesel gensets across 2,130 remote sites, with electricity costs at 2–30× the national average and ~3 MtCO₂ emissions annually. Gili Ketapang exemplifies the urgency: dense population, high CEPC, and unreliable water supply.

De-risked Dual-Entity Framework

Renewable & Hydrogen Co. (RHC)

• 2 MWp Floating PV, 900 kW PEM Electrolyzer, 176 kW Fuel Cell backup
• Business model: PPA + IaaS with performance-based O&M
• Revenues: Grid sales, H₂ service fees, carbon credits

Flexible Water Co. (FWC)

• 800 m³/day SWRO with WHR integration, potable + ultrapure output
• Business model: BLT with PDAM + O&M service fees
• Revenues: Lease payments, service fees

Technical Readiness

All subsystems are commercially proven: FPV, PEM electrolysis, pressurized H₂ storage (300 bar), stationary fuel cells, and SWRO with WHR. System engineered for ≥95% availability and modular scaling.

Policy Alignment

Backed by PLN's dedieselisasi, Presidential Regulation 122/2022 and RUPTL roadmap. Contract models (PPA, BLT, IaaS) ensure utility oversight and cost predictability.

Implementation Roadmap (24 months)

Phase 0 — Months 1–6

Secure PPA & BLT; E&S assessments; stakeholder engagement; financing close.

Phase 1 — Months 7–18

FPV EPC; electrolyzer & HFCG install; SWRO construction; grid integration; storage commissioning.

Phase 3 — Year 3+

Template replication to additional islands; portfolio financing; continuous optimization.

Risk Landscape

Mitigations
